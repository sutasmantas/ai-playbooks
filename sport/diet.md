# Option C: Gut-First — 7-Day Plan
*Revised 2026-06-29: 2 scoops whey daily, chicken removed, blueberries built in.*

---

## Why Option C
Wastyk et al. (2021) showed that a high-fermented-food diet increased microbiome diversity and reduced 19 inflammatory proteins — but only when fermented foods were eaten consistently and in high volume. Greek yogurt and varškė together deliver two distinct bacterial strains every single day. Fiber (30–36g/day) feeds those bacteria. This plan keeps both mechanisms running daily without recipes or variety.

Three additional evidence-backed changes (adversarial 3-vote verified, 2026-06-29):
- **Olive oil 35ml/day:** PREDIMED trial shows T3 threshold (~34.6g fat/day from oil) is the first dose with a significant CVD signal; T2 (~19.5g/day) was null vs control. Previous 15–20ml sat in the null zone.
- **Walnuts instead of peanut butter:** PREDIMED tree-nut arm HR 0.68 for MACE. Peanuts were explicitly excluded from that arm. Absolute CVD benefit is small at this age (NNT >800) but the swap is calorie-neutral and directionally positive. Walnuts also provide omega-3.
- **Oily fish 1x/week (Friday):** UK Biobank HR 0.93 all-cause mortality / HR 0.85 CVD-specific mortality at exactly 1 serving/week. Benefit is null at ≥2 servings — Friday only.

---

## Hard Constraints
- Protein: 140–185g/day
- Calories: ~1900–2400 kcal/day (training days at upper end)
- Fat: 55g+ /day
- Fiber: 30–36g/day
- Fermented: 3 servings/day minimum (yogurt + varškė, both every day)

## Portion Guide — Non-Weighed Foods

| Food | Grams | In human terms |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Carrots | 150g | 2 medium carrots — eat raw |
| Cabbage | 150g | roughly a quarter of a small white head, sliced thin |
| Frozen blueberries | 150g | a heaped mug, or fills your cupped hands |
| Cooked potatoes | 200g | 2 medium potatoes |
| Drained beans | 200g | ¾ of a standard 400g can |
| Oats (dry) | 80g | a big cup — about 5–6 heaped tablespoons |
| Milk | 200ml | one standard glass |
| Walnuts (plain) | 30g | small handful — roughly 10–12 halves |
| Olive oil | 35ml | 2 tablespoons + 1 teaspoon — measure once to calibrate your pour |
| Varškė | 250g | half a 500g tub |
| Greek yogurt | 200g | 1 standard pot (most pots are sold as 200g) |
| Oily fish (sardines/mackerel) | 100g | 1 standard tin drained |

**Cabbage prep note:** raw white cabbage tastes noticeably bitter/sulfurous to a lot of people — that's normal, not a bad head. Steaming fixes it (already an approved swap, see the meal tables). Steam sliced cabbage **4–6 min, just until tender** — stop there. Cruciferous veg gets *more* sulfurous the longer it cooks, so oversteaming makes the smell/taste worse, not better. If it's still bitter after a short steam, a squeeze of lemon or splash of vinegar right after cuts the remaining bitterness; salting and letting it sit 10 min before eating (raw or steamed) also helps. Skip the tough white core — that's the most bitter part.

## The 4 Meals — Same Structure Every Day

| Meal | Time | What |
|------|------|------|
| Meal 1 — Morning bowl | Within 1 hr of waking | Yogurt + oats + milk + whey scoop 1 + walnuts + banana + blueberries (training days only) |
| Meal 2 — Lunch bowl | ~4–5 hrs after Meal 1 | Varškė + beans + cabbage + oil |
| Shake | Any time — afternoon or evening | Whey scoop 2 in water |
| Dinner | After training / evening | Eggs + potatoes (training days only) + carrots |

## 7-Day Plan

---

### Monday — Upper A (Strength) — TRAINING DAY

**Meal 1 — Morning (~8–9am)**

| Food | Amount | In human terms |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Greek yogurt 0% | 200g | 1 pot |
| Rolled oats (dry) | 80g | a big cup, ~5–6 heaped tbsp |
| Milk semi-skimmed | 200ml | one glass |
| Whey protein (scoop 1) | 40g | 1 scoop — stir into bowl |
| Walnuts (plain, no salt) | 30g | 2 heaped tablespoons |
| Banana | 1 (120g) | 1 banana |
| Frozen blueberries | 150g | a heaped mug |

*Overnight method: mix oats into the yogurt + milk the night before, leave in fridge. In the morning stir in whey, add walnuts, banana, blueberries. Eat the whole bowl.*

**Meal 2 — Lunch (~12–2pm)**

| Food | Amount | In human terms |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Liesa varškė | 250g | half a 500g tub |
| Canned beans, drained | 200g | ¾ of a 400g can |
| Cabbage, raw or steamed | 150g | a quarter of a small white head |
| Olive oil | 35ml | 2 tablespoons + 1 teaspoon |

*Open tub of varškė, drain and rinse beans over it, slice raw cabbage alongside, drizzle oil. No cooking needed.*

**Shake — any time (afternoon or evening)**

Whey scoop 2 (40g) in water. Timing doesn't matter — total daily protein is what drives the adaptation, not proximity to any specific set.

**Dinner (~7–9pm)**

| Food | Amount | In human terms |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Eggs | 3 | boil, fry, or scramble |
| Potatoes, boiled | 200g | 2 medium potatoes |
| Carrots, raw | 150g | 2 medium carrots |

*Tip: boil 9–15 eggs at the start of the week. Keep in fridge. Just peel and eat.*

**Monday totals: ~179g P · ~2350 kcal · ~79g fat · ~36g fiber ✓**
*Fermented: yogurt (Meal 1) + varškė (Meal 2) = 3 servings ✓*

### Friday — Session C + HIIT + Oily Fish — Rest Day Meals

**Meal 1:** Same as Sunday (no blueberries). **Meal 2:** Varškė + beans + cabbage + 35ml oil. **Shake:** Whey scoop 2 in water.

**Dinner — fish swap (1x/week only)**

| Food | Amount | In human terms |

| Egg | 1 | boiled or fried alongside |

*Swaps 2 of the 3 usual eggs for a tin of oily fish. Near-neutral calories. Evidence: UK Biobank HR 0.93 all-cause mortality / HR 0.85 CVD-specific mortality at 1 serving/week. Benefit is null at ≥2x/week — Friday only, not Tuesday.*

**Friday totals: ~186g P · ~2160 kcal · ~80g fat · ~30g fiber ✓**

## Supplements

**Every day (training and rest):**
- Creatine: 5g with any meal
- Vitamin D3: 2000–4000 IU with a fat-containing meal (Meals 1, 2, or dinner all work)
- Magnesium glycinate or citrate: 300–400mg in the evening

**Before any training session (Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat):**
- Caffeine: 3–6mg per kg bodyweight, 45–60 min before the session. Skip after 2pm if it affects your sleep.
