# Option A: 2-Meal Minimalist
*Two large meals per day. No breakfast. Zero mid-day food decisions. Generated 2026-06-29.*

---

## WHY THIS VARIANT
Schoenfeld (2015, meta-analysis of 15 RCTs) found no body composition difference between 2-3 and 5-6 meals per day when total calories and protein are equated, making the minimalist 2-meal structure fully supported by evidence. Reducing daily food decisions to two fixed events eliminates decision fatigue and the cumulative tracking errors that accumulate across frequent small meals. All hard constraints (140-175g protein, 1900-2100 kcal, 55-65g fat, 30-35g fiber) are achievable within two large meals.

## PRACTICAL NOTES

### Hunger before noon
The first week will involve hunger between waking and Meal 1. This is adaptation, not deficit. Strategies:
- Black coffee (no calories) is permitted and suppresses appetite effectively
- Drink 500ml water immediately on waking
- Keep busy until noon — hunger peaks then fades; waiting it out is the skill to build
- If hunger is genuinely severe (dizzy, unable to focus), add a 20g whey shake in water (~80 kcal, 16g protein). This is a rescue, not a daily habit. Do not add food systematically — that defeats the variant.

### Training timing by day
- Monday, Tuesday, Saturday (morning sessions): train fasted at 11:00, eat Meal 1 at noon. Caffeine at 10:15 covers the fasted window. Post-workout hunger makes Meal 1 easier to finish.
- Thursday, Friday (afternoon sessions): train at 17:00, Meal 1 at noon pre-fuels the session, Meal 2 post-refuels. No fasted training needed.
- Wednesday (cardio): morning shake at 09:00 is the protein bridge before the uncontrolled restaurant lunch. Cardio is low intensity — fasted is fine, but if energy is low, the shake also covers this.
- Sunday: no training, no timing pressure.

### What to do if hunger is severe
Severity scale:
1. Mild (slightly aware of hunger) — ignore, drink water or black coffee
2. Moderate (distracted but functional) — wait, this is adaptation; passes within 15-20 min
3. Severe (dizzy, shaky, cannot concentrate) — take the 20g whey rescue shake; log it; if this happens more than 2 days in a week, bump Meal 1 back to 11:00 instead of noon

### Fat floor compliance
The 55g fat floor is the most commonly missed constraint on 2-meal structures. Default rescue: add 15ml olive oil to any meal (+135 kcal, +15g fat). Keep a bottle on the table. If you are systematically under 55g, add peanut butter to Meal 1 before adjusting anything else.

### Supplement placement summary
- Creatine 3-5g: Meal 1 (any day, including rest days)
- Vitamin D 2000-4000 IU: Meal 2 (fat always present)
- Magnesium 300-400mg glycinate/citrate: Meal 2, last thing (evening timing supports sleep)
- Caffeine 3-6mg/kg: training days only, 45-60 min pre-session (not taken on Wednesday cardio or Sunday)

### Wednesday restaurant strategy
Wednesday is the structurally weakest day because Meal 1 is uncontrolled. The morning shake is mandatory, not optional — it locks in 32g protein before control is lost. At the restaurant: order any grilled/roasted meat main, ask for no cream sauces, skip bread and dessert, any vegetable side is fine. If the restaurant only has pasta/pizza options, order the meatiest option available and accept a lower protein Meal 1; Meal 2 compensates.
