# Option B: High-Fat, Carbs Around Training Only
*Fat as primary energy on rest days. Carbs concentrated around training sessions. Generated 2026-06-29.*

## CONFIRMED UPDATE (2026-06-29)

**Add on training days (Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat): 150-200g frozen blueberries** mixed into existing oats or yogurt. Polyphenols confirmed to reduce DOMS (d = -0.44) and MVC recovery loss (d = -0.78) after adversarial 3-vote research. ~85-110 kcal, ~20-27g carbs — on training days this fits within the higher-carb allocation. On rest days blueberries are not needed (no back-to-back training recovery to support).

---

## WHY THIS VARIANT
Sacks 2009 (n=811, 2-year RCT) confirmed no meaningful long-term fat loss difference between 35% and 65% carb diets when calories are equated, making high-fat rest days metabolically equivalent for fat loss. Whittaker 2021 establishes 55-65g/day fat as a hard floor to protect testosterone during deficit, so rest-day fat is pushed to 85-95g to stay well above this threshold while dropping carbs to 60-80g. Carbs are retained on training days (150-200g) to fuel performance without compromising the fat loss deficit.

---

## HARD CONSTRAINTS APPLIED
- Protein: 140-175g/day (Helms 2014 + 2025 meta-regression)
- Calories: 1900-2100 kcal/day
- Fat minimum: 55-65g/day floor (Whittaker 2021)
- Fiber: 30-35g/day (Howarth 2001)

## WHAT THIS FEELS LIKE

Practical differences from a standard moderate-carb diet:

**Sunday is noticeably different.** No oats, no potatoes, no rice, no banana. Breakfast is eggs and peanut butter and cottage cheese — heavier, more filling, no sweet/starchy carbs. Hunger is blunted but the meal feels "heavier" early in the day.

**Training mornings feel identical.** Oats pre-session and a shake post-session are the same as any moderate-carb approach. The difference is invisible on those mornings.

**Post-training meals are slightly smaller on carbs than a high-carb plan** — 150-200g cooked starch rather than 250-300g — but protein and fat make up the satiety deficit. The meals feel complete, not restricted.

**Hunger is lower on Sunday** than on a high-carb rest day. Fat slows gastric emptying. The four-egg breakfast with peanut butter holds for 4-5 hours without a snack.

**Peanut butter appears every day** as the primary fat lever. On rest days it doubles (40-60g total across meals). This is practical for a budget Lithuanian grocery shop.

**Beans anchor the rest day.** 150-200g cooked kidney beans is the only significant carb source on Sunday. This also delivers the highest single-day fiber load of the week.

**The week does not feel "low carb."** On six of seven days, carbs are 140-200g. Only Sunday drops below 100g. Anyone expecting a ketogenic experience will not get one.
