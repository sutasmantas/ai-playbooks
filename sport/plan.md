## Full Body Workout

Rep ranges rotate across sessions — same exercises, different weight and reps. Heavy low-rep work builds strength (neural), lighter higher-rep work builds size (muscular). Both run together (DUP).

*Bone density note: moderate-weight, higher-rep lifting 3x/week outperforms heavy low-rep for bone density (Wang 2023). Sessions B and C provide this. Session A is heavier — intentional for strength.*

*To save ~10 min:* superset pull-ups + floor press (one set each, then rest). Same for rows + OHP.

## Cardio — HIIT (Tue + Fri + Sat) + Zone 2 from commute

**HIIT — Tue + Fri + Sat:** THENX routine or equivalent, same format you were already doing. 3 sessions/week addresses the total weekly volume needed for cardiac remodeling.

**Progression target:** Work up to 20–25 min per session over 6–8 weeks. Start extending Tue and Sat first — Friday is combined with lifting so keep that one shorter until the others feel easy.

**Wednesday — Zone 2 fully covered:** Your 35-min walk to work + 35-min walk back = 70 min of brisk walking at Zone 2 pace. This entirely meets the longevity threshold from Arem et al. 2015 (JAMA Internal Medicine, n=661,137) — the largest mortality step-function benefit comes from going from zero to 1–2× the minimum, which your commute already achieves. No additional cardio needed on Wednesday.

## Progression Rule

**Hit the top of the rep range on all sets → add weight next session and rebuild from the bottom.**

Example: Session B at 8–12. Hit 12/12/12 at 30kg → next Session B try 32kg, start at 8. Session A at 5–6. Hit 6/6/6 → add weight.

Each session type progresses independently — Session A weight is always heavier than B, B is always heavier than C.

**Pull-ups with band:**
1. Both feet in band → target 3×10
2. One foot in band → target 3×10
3. Unassisted

## Deload — Every 4–5 Weeks

**Research basis:** Pelland 2025, Bernárdez-Vázquez 2022. Systematic recovery prevents cumulative fatigue from masking strength gains.

After every 4–5 weeks of training: keep all sessions, keep the same weights, **cut sets by 40–50%** (2 sets instead of 3, 1 set instead of 2). Do not go to failure. One week, then resume normally.

You will feel like you're undertraining. That's correct — the goal is recovery, not stimulus.

---

## Overtraining Signals — Stop Before You Break

Research identified 5 reliable early signals (ranked by reliability):

1. **Resting HR elevated 5–7+ bpm above your normal** for 2+ consecutive mornings — check this 30 sec after waking
2. **Performance regression** on the same lifts across 2+ consecutive sessions with no obvious cause
3. **Sleep quality degrading** — waking at night, feeling unrested despite hours
4. **Increased minor illness or joint aches** — more colds, nagging shoulders/knees
5. **HRV trending down** over 5–7 days (HRV4Training app if you want to track this)

**Two of these simultaneously = implement deload immediately, don't wait for the 4–5 week mark.**

---

## If Exhausted

Drop to 2 lifting sessions that week. Stop 4–5 reps short of failure on every set. Skip one HIIT. Resume normally the next week.

## Form Notes

### Dumbbell Rows
No bench. Free hand on same-side thigh, just above the knee.

- **Cue: elbow to back pocket** — not "pull the weight up"
- Dumbbell travels toward hip, not shoulder
- Shoulder blade forward at the bottom, retract hard at the top — 1 sec squeeze
- Forearm vertical throughout
- Feel it in arms more than back → weight too heavy, drop it

### Overhead Press
- Start at shoulder height, elbows at ~45° forward of your body (not flared out sideways)
- Press straight up — bar or dumbbells finish directly above ears, not in front
- At the top: arms locked out, biceps near or touching your ears
- Do not lean back as the weight gets heavy — that turns it into an incline press and loads the lower back

### Bulgarian Split Squats / Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift — Post-Set Impairment (informational, own call on next steps)
Reported pattern (2026-07-02): lightheadedness and impaired thinking after heavy leg sets, scaling with weight added, sometimes lasting up to **30 minutes** — much less after other heavy exercises (e.g. overhead press), and described as feeling similar to post-cardio fatigue. Not explained by breathing technique (already breathing deeply/well through sets; pace also doesn't degrade the way it would with normal fatigue). Most likely tied to legs recruiting far more total muscle mass than upper-body exercises, which drives a much larger acute cardiovascular response — consistent with the post-cardio comparison. Worth a doctor's read whenever it's checked, given the load-dependence and duration, but timing/decision on that is not mine to gate. If useful data is wanted for that conversation later: heart rate/blood pressure captured before, during, and in the following 30 min (smartwatch/home cuff) would be more informative than a post-hoc description.

### Farmer's Carry
Why it's here: grip strength under load (grip is a strong longevity predictor — your gripper trains it too, the carry compounds it while moving), anti-lateral-flexion endurance (the core resisting the weight pulling you sideways), and loaded locomotion. Not prescribed for bone density — BSS and SLRDL already provide the axial compression that matters.

- Pick a weight that challenges your grip within 40 sec (heavy, not moderate)
- Stand tall — do not let the weight drag your shoulder down
- Small steps, braced core, don't hold your breath — full exhale every 3–4 steps
- Walk in a straight line; turn around when you run out of space
- If no room to walk: stand in place and hold for 40 sec (loaded isometric grip + anti-lateral hold)
- Progress by increasing weight, not duration

### Face Pulls
- Anchor at face height
- Pull toward nose — pulling lower turns it into a row
- Elbows at or above shoulder height throughout
- Finish hands flared out and back, fully externally rotated
