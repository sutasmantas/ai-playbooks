## WAVE A — Health perspective: Diet

What the current diet optimised for: gut microbiome (Wastyk), hormonal health (fat floor, D3), fiber/satiety, sport performance.
What was NOT researched from a health perspective:

| Angle | Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Cardiovascular dietary patterns | Does the current diet match what reduces CVD risk? PREDIMED (n=7,447), Lyon Diet Heart Study | Single largest evidence base in nutrition; our diet was never designed against it |
| Ultra-processed foods | Does the current plan inadvertently include UPFs? Hall 2019 metabolic ward (+500 kcal/day spontaneous intake on UPF) | Plan avoids worst offenders but not from a research basis |
| Omega-3 from food | Oily fish (sardines, mackerel) evidence for inflammation + CVD — prior omega-3 claims all failed verification due to citation errors, not because evidence doesn't exist | Deserves a clean re-run from food sources, not supplement framing |
| Longevity dietary patterns | Blue Zones, Mediterranean, DASH — which dietary pattern has the strongest all-cause mortality data? | User's goal includes health — longevity research is the relevant lens |
| Cancer risk from diet | Red/processed meat (WHO IARC Group 1/2A), cruciferous vegetables, fiber — does current plan reduce or increase risk? | Not covered at all |
| Blood pressure / sodium | DASH trial evidence; current plan's sodium load | Not researched |

**Influence on diet.md:** Will likely add oily fish as a swap option, possibly adjust the snack list, possibly confirm/challenge the current food choices from a different angle.

## WAVE B — Health perspective: Exercise

What the current plan optimised for: hypertrophy (Pelland 2025), strength (DUP/LP), VO2 max (HIIT), grip (mortality predictor).
What was NOT researched from a health perspective:

| Angle | Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| All-cause mortality and exercise dose | What volume/type of exercise has the strongest longevity signal? (Lee et al., Stamatakis "weekend warrior" data) | Current plan's 3 lifting + 2 HIIT — is that the right structure for health, not just hypertrophy? |
| Cardiovascular health from exercise | HIIT vs Zone 2 for cardiac health specifically — not VO2 max but actual CVD risk reduction | User identified cardio as weak point; CVD is a health goal, not just fitness |
| Bone density | Resistance training effects on BMD — which exercises produce the best bone stimulus? Are SLRDL and BSS sufficient? | Long-term bone health, especially relevant as user ages |
| Mental health / depression / anxiety | Aerobic vs resistance training for mental wellbeing | Not researched at all; health goal includes this |
| Brain health / dementia prevention | Exercise type and cognitive reserve — BDNF, hippocampal volume | Long-term health |
| Systemic inflammation from exercise | Does the current 3-day lifting + 2-day HIIT pattern reduce or increase inflammatory markers vs alternative structures? | Connects to diet's Wastyk findings — exercise and fermented foods may compound |
| Sleep quality from exercise | Which exercise timing and type produces the best sleep? | Sleep is a health multiplier |
| Injury risk | Bulgarian split squats and SLRDL injury rates for beginners — is this the safest lower-body option? | Plan should be sustainable without injury |

**Influence on plan.md:** May change cardio prescription, add or swap exercises, change session timing recommendations.
