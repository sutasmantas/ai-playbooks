Second, sharper refinement: even among money-vulnerabilities, exclude the ones an attacker couldn't actually get away with — every account on this platform is KYC'd (real name/national ID/phone/address/bank transfer at registration), so anything that produces a visibly implausible outcome (a car "selling" for a few euros, a win with no real bid history) is self-reporting and traces straight back to a real identity. ## Next, once tools are available again (reordered 2026-07-01 by attacker-viability, see above)

**Top priority — the two things actually worth an attacker's risk:**
1. **max_bet leak check (#5)** — with two accounts, see if account B's proxy ceiling is ever visible to account A through any response. The single most valuable thing to confirm or rule out.
2. **Race condition, "who wins" form (#3b)** — fire simultaneous same-price bids (Burp Repeater parallel-send or Turbo Intruder) and see whether the winner among comparable bids is exploitable, *without* trying to win below the true current price (that variant is the not-viable one).
3. **(session 4, new, no account needed)** **Ceiling-inference pass over `auction_bids.php`'s already-public closed-auction bid ladders.** Research found eBay's proxy-bid math is publicly gameable at the margins — an attentive party can sometimes narrow a rival's true `max_bet` from how the displayed price jumps after each new bid, since the price shown is `min(new bidder's max, previous leader's max + increment)`. This platform's `auction_bids.php` already discloses the exact same kind of step-by-step auto-bid ladder (amount + timestamp + `(A)` auto-bid marker) for any closed auction, anonymously, right now (see FINDINGS.md). Worth collecting several more closed-auction ladders (same passive technique already used for auction 23500) and checking whether the increment pattern ever reveals a proxy's ceiling being reached (a bidder's `(A)` responses stopping mid-auction while price kept climbing from someone else, rather than the auction simply ending) — this would upgrade #5 from "needs an account to test" to "already demonstrable from public data."
