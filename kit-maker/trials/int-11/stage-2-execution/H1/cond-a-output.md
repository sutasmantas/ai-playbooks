# Research Angles: Periodization for Marathon Performance

Topic: How to periodize training to improve marathon race performance.

---

## Angle 1: Classical Periodization Vocabulary (Matveyev/Soviet tradition)

The founding periodization vocabulary comes from Soviet sports science (Matveyev, Verkhoshansky). Much endurance literature still uses this terminology. A search that skips these terms misses foundational papers and meta-analyses that use them as controlled vocabulary.

Queries:
- `"periodization" "marathon" "macrocycle" OR "mesocycle" training adaptation`
- `"annual training plan" marathon running performance physiological`
- `Matveyev periodization endurance running long-distance`

---

## Angle 2: Block Periodization (Issurin model)

Block periodization concentrates highly specific stimuli into short mesocycles ("blocks") rather than developing all qualities simultaneously. Issurin's model is distinct from traditional periodization and has generated its own literature stream. Searching only "periodization" misses papers that frame their work explicitly against this model.

Queries:
- `"block periodization" endurance running marathon performance`
- `Issurin "concentrated loads" running OR endurance training`
- `"accumulation block" OR "transmutation block" OR "realization block" marathon`

---

## Angle 3: Intensity Distribution / Polarized Training Model

Stephen Seiler's polarized training model (roughly 80% low intensity, 20% high intensity, minimal threshold work) has generated substantial empirical literature distinct from periodization framing. Papers in this tradition rarely use "periodization" as their primary keyword — they use "intensity distribution" or "training zone" vocabulary instead.

Queries:
- `"polarized training" OR "polarised training" marathon endurance performance`
- `"intensity distribution" running "low intensity" "high intensity" adaptation`
- `Seiler "80/20" OR "pyramidal" OR "polarized" endurance running`

---

## Angle 4: Training Load Monitoring Vocabulary (Foster / ATL-CTL tradition)

Carl Foster's "training monotony" and "training strain" model, and the acute:chronic workload ratio (ACWR) literature from sports medicine, approach periodization through load quantification rather than program structure. This vocabulary is common in sports science and injury-prevention journals that a performance-focused search would miss.

Queries:
- `"acute:chronic workload ratio" OR "ACWR" running marathon injury performance`
- `"training load" "session RPE" running periodization adaptation`
- `Foster "training monotony" "training strain" endurance marathon`

---

## Angle 5: Tapering and Peaking Literature

The taper (pre-race reduction in training load) is a sub-domain of periodization with its own dense literature. Papers on taper optimization rarely foreground "periodization" as a keyword. Missing this stream means missing substantial evidence on the final 2-4 weeks of a marathon training block.

Queries:
- `"taper" OR "tapering" marathon running performance meta-analysis`
- `"pre-competition taper" "training volume" reduction endurance performance`
- `"peaking" strategy marathon running physiological markers`

---

## Angle 6: Physiological Target Vocabulary (VO2max / Lactate Threshold framing)

Some periodization research is indexed under the physiological marker being trained rather than under "periodization." Papers may focus on "lactate threshold training blocks" or "VO2max development phases" without using periodization terminology.

Queries:
- `"lactate threshold" training phase block marathon performance`
- `"VO2max" development "training phase" long-distance running periodization`
- `"running economy" training adaptation "training period" marathon`

---

## Angle 7: Overtraining and Overreaching (Failure-Case Angle)

The overtraining and non-functional overreaching literature represents the failure mode of periodization done poorly. This stream identifies what breaks when periodization principles are violated, making it a source of implicit design principles. Papers here use "overtraining syndrome," "functional overreaching," and "HRV-guided training" vocabulary.

Queries:
- `"overtraining syndrome" OR "non-functional overreaching" marathon endurance running`
- `"HRV-guided training" OR "heart rate variability" periodization endurance running`
- `"training maladaptation" long-distance running prevention periodization`

---

## Angle 8: Adjacent Field — Cycling Periodization

Cycling has power meters and thus a richer quantitative periodization literature than running. The physiological mechanisms transfer directly (VO2max, lactate threshold, aerobic capacity). Searching cycling periodization with "endurance" as a filter surfaces methods applicable to marathon that may not yet have running-specific validation studies.

Queries:
- `periodization cycling endurance "power output" performance adaptation transferable`
- `"training periodization" cycling VO2max lactate threshold "aerobic capacity"`
- `"base training" "build phase" "peak phase" cycling endurance physiology`

---

## Angle 9: Strength Training Periodization Cross-Application

Strength and conditioning literature has more controlled periodization trials than running literature (easier to isolate variables in a gym setting). The wave-loading, undulating periodization, and linear vs. non-linear periodization debates from S&C may apply to endurance training volume/intensity structure.

Queries:
- `"undulating periodization" OR "non-linear periodization" endurance aerobic performance`
- `"linear periodization" versus "undulating periodization" endurance running adaptation`
- `"strength endurance" periodization marathon running concurrent training`

---

## Angle 10: Null-Hypothesis / Skeptical Angle — Does Periodization Outperform Consistent Training?

A standard search surfaces pro-periodization papers. A targeted skeptical search finds trials that compare periodized programs to non-periodized continuous training with matched volumes. These studies constrain the effect size claims and prevent overfitting the training plan to theoretical models.

Queries:
- `periodization versus "continuous training" OR "non-periodized" endurance running comparison`
- `"does periodization improve" endurance running randomized controlled trial`
- `"training structure" effect marathon performance "matched volume" comparison`

---

## Angle 11: Individual Response Heterogeneity

Response to periodized training is not uniform. Papers studying "responders vs. non-responders," genetic variation in trainability, and individual optimization of periodization parameters are indexed under exercise genomics and precision sports science vocabulary — not under "periodization."

Queries:
- `"individual response" OR "responder" OR "non-responder" endurance training periodization`
- `"trainability" genetic variation endurance marathon aerobic adaptation`
- `"personalized training" OR "individualized periodization" endurance running performance`
