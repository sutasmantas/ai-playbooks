## Angle 3 ‚Äî Central Governor Model and Neuroscience of Pacing

Tim Noakes' central governor framework reframes pacing as a centrally regulated protective response, not a purely metabolic phenomenon. This tradition uses different vocabulary and appears in neuroscience and integrative physiology journals.

- `"central governor" AND pacing AND running`
- `"anticipatory regulation" AND fatigue AND endurance performance`
- `"teleoanticipation" AND exercise pacing`

## Angle 4 ‚Äî Perceived Exertion and Psychophysiological Regulation

- `"perceived exertion" AND pacing AND "long-distance" AND running`
- `RPE AND "self-paced" AND endurance AND running`
- `"effort perception" AND "pace regulation" AND running competition`

## Angle 5 ‚Äî Optimal Control and Mathematical Optimization

Engineering and applied mathematics literature models pacing as an optimal control problem ‚Äî minimizing race time subject to physiological constraints. This tradition is almost invisible to standard sports science searches.

- `"optimal pacing" AND running AND "optimal control"`
- `"energy management" AND running AND optimization AND race`
- `"dynamic programming" OR "calculus of variations" AND running AND pacing`

## Angle 6 ‚Äî Failure-Case and Wall-Hitting Literature

Research centered on why pacing goes wrong: glycogen depletion ("hitting the wall"), premature fatigue, and catastrophic slowdown in the final race segment. Failure-case literature often surfaces mechanisms that success-case studies obscure.

- `"hitting the wall" AND marathon AND pacing`
- `"glycogen depletion" AND marathon AND performance collapse`
- `"end-spurt" OR "end spurt" AND running AND pacing failure`

## Angle 7 ‚Äî Tactical and Pack Running Literature

Sports tactics research that treats pacing as a competitive game-theoretic decision rather than an individual physiological optimization. Focuses on drafting, surging, and responses to rivals. Common in coaching science and tactical sports journals.

- `"pack running" AND tactics AND distance running`
- `"drafting" AND running AND "pace strategy"`
- `"surging" AND middle-distance OR long-distance AND competition AND pacing`

## Angle 8 ‚Äî Environmental and Course-Profile Interaction

## Angle 10 ‚Äî Elite vs. Recreational Athlete Stratification

## Angle 11 ‚Äî Comparative Biology and Animal Locomotion

Evolutionary and comparative biology treats pacing as an energy allocation problem that predates human sport. - `"optimal locomotion" AND energy AND endurance AND animal`
