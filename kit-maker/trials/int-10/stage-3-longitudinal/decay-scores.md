## Methodological Notes

**Tradition count methodology:** Counted all distinct tradition labels as declared in (1) the Coverage Map section and (2) the Angle List tradition labels. Where a Coverage Map tradition was "integrated into" the C1 slot, it was counted once as one tradition. Disconfirming variants (e.g., "NLP prompting / disconfirming") were treated as the same parent tradition as their base. This produces the most accurate count of conceptual territory covered.

**Repetition rate methodology:** Evaluated under both strict exact-label matching and lenient root-concept grouping. Under strict matching, repetition rate is effectively 0% throughout. Under lenient grouping, maximum observed rate is 33% at L9 (driven by "Cognitive science" and "LLM evaluation" parent concepts appearing in ≥3 prior inputs each). Both interpretations produce a clear PASS. The lenient calculation is reported as the conservative bound.

**L9 null-hypothesis qualification:** The threshold as written measures final output, not initial generation. The gate-summary pass is part of the kit's production pipeline. The angle exists in the output, satisfying the threshold condition. However, the initial omission is a process signal: null-hypothesis angles require structural elicitation for reliable generation. This aligns with the cross-batch notes observation and documented kit design guidance.

**Tradition count range observation:** Expected range is 3–5; observed range is 5–6. This does not trigger a threshold cross (trigger is <3). The consistent overage may reflect: (a) the adversarial and cross-disciplinary mandatory slots reliably adding 2 traditions beyond the confirmatory baseline, and (b) Coverage Map traditions that are "partially integrated" still being counted. This is not a decay signal but may warrant a calibration note if the expected range needs updating.
