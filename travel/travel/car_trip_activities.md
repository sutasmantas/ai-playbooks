# Car Trip Activities

Games and activities for 4 guys (~26 years old) on an ~8-hour drive. Board games are covered separately; this is verbal games, card games, phone games, deduction games, challenges, and stop activities.

## How To Read This

Every game uses the same format so it can be browsed, filtered, or built into a picker:

- **Metadata line** — one line per game with these fields:
  - **Players** — how many it needs (e.g. `4`, `2-4`, `2 at a time`).
  - **Where** — `In car` or `At a stop`.
  - **Driver** — `Plays` (talk-only, safe to join), `Sets up then plays`, `Judges only`, or `Excluded`.
  - **Energy** — `Low` (works when tired), `Medium`, or `High` (awake and loud).
  - **Setup** — `None`, `Phone (assign roles)`, `Phone (look up)`, `Phone (music)`, or `Deck of cards`.
  - **Type** — Conversation, Debate, Deduction, Bluffing, Performance, Guessing, Word, Memory, Reflex, Card, Scoring, Photo, Stop, or Challenge.
- **How to play** — the steps.
- **Win / lose** — how a round ends (some conversation games have no winner; that's stated).
- **Bank** — the ready-made prompts/options to pick from, so nothing has to be invented.

**Win / lose:** most points by arrival wins.

**Bank (what to award points for):**

## Competitive Games

### Playlist Battle

**Players** 3-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays (says pick aloud, a passenger queues it) · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** Phone (music) · **Type** Scoring

**How to play:**

1. Pick a theme for the round.
2. Each player has 90 seconds to queue one song that fits the theme, secretly. The driver says their pick to a passenger who queues it.
3. Play the first 30 seconds of each pick. The picker gets ten words max to sell it.
4. Everyone scores each song 1-5 (not their own).

**Win / lose:** highest total wins the round and a Trip Scoreboard point.

**Bank (themes):**

- best driving song
- song that goes hard but you're embarrassed to admit it
- best song to scream in a car
- a song that instantly takes you back to school
- worst song that is somehow still a banger
- song most likely to start an argument
- best one-hit wonder
- a song your dad would love
- best song under two minutes
- song that should be illegal to skip

### Auction Game

**Players** 3-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays (a passenger tracks budgets) · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Scoring

**How to play:** one person is the auctioneer (rotate each round) and reads a fixed list of items. Everyone has 100 imaginary euros and no single bid can exceed 40. Auction items one at a time; highest bidder wins each and pays from their budget. A passenger who isn't the auctioneer calls out remaining budgets. When all items are sold, each owner makes a 10-second case for their best item.

**Win / lose:** the car votes for the single best item of the round (not their own); its owner takes the point.

**Bank (ready item lists, auctioneer reads one):**

- **Superpowers:** flight, invisibility, super strength, reading minds, teleportation, stopping time.
- **Best thing to have for free forever:** fuel, coffee, flights, pizza, concert tickets, taxis.
- **Apocalypse teammates:** a doctor, a mechanic, a chef, a fighter, a farmer, a comedian.
- **Best car to own:** an old reliable hatchback, a sports car, a massive pickup, a camper van, a classic 90s car, an electric luxury sedan.
- **Best decade to live in:** 60s, 70s, 80s, 90s, 2000s, 2010s.
- **Dinner-party guests (any era):** a famous comedian, a world leader, a scientist, a footballer, a rockstar, a chef.
- **Best fast food:** burger, pizza, fried chicken, kebab, sushi, tacos.
- **Best pet:** dog, cat, parrot, snake, hamster, fish.
- **Best holiday:** beach resort, city break, ski trip, road trip, safari, festival.
- **Best movie franchise:** Star Wars, Marvel, James Bond, Lord of the Rings, Fast & Furious, Harry Potter.

## Deduction And Social Games

### Spyfall-Style Location

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Sets up then plays (a passenger deals roles) · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** Phone (assign roles) · **Type** Deduction

**How to play:** one player is the spy and doesn't know the location; everyone else shares the same location (a passenger deals it by phone message). Players take turns asking each other questions about the place. Answers must prove you know where you are without naming it — too vague and you look like the spy, too obvious and the spy learns the location. Run a 5-minute timer.

**Win / lose:** any player can call a vote to accuse someone. Catch the spy → group wins. Accuse an innocent, or the spy correctly guesses the location → spy wins.

**Bank (probe questions):**

- "Would you bring a kid here?"
- "How are you dressed right now?"
- "Roughly how much money do you have on you?"
- "Is it loud or quiet where you are?"
- "Have you been here before?"
- "Are you here for work or for fun?"
- "What's the smell like?"
- "Could you fall asleep here?"
- "Are there a lot of people around?"
- "What time of day is it, would you say?"
- "Is there anyone here you'd want to avoid?"
- "How did you get here?"

**Bank (locations):**

### 20 Questions: Hard Mode

**Players** 2+ · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Deduction

**How to play:** one person thinks of a person, place, object, brand, movie, game, event, or food. Others get 20 yes/no questions, plus one of the hard-mode rules below.

**Win / lose:** guess it within the limit → guessers win; run out → the thinker wins.

**Bank (hard modes):**

### Mafia-Lite

**Players** 4+ (better with 5+) · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays (a passenger or app moderates) · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** Phone (app or role messages) · **Type** Deduction

**How to play:** classic Mafia wants ~6+ players plus a moderator, so at 4 it's thin — one elimination and the mafia is obvious. Two ways to make it work: use a Mafia/Werewolf phone app as the moderator so all four play, or save it for when you're more than four. Setup at 4: 1 mafia, 1 detective, 2 civilians. Each night the mafia silently picks someone and the detective checks one person; each day everyone argues and votes someone out.

**Win / lose:** mafia wins if last standing; town wins if it votes out the mafia.

**Note:** for hidden-role fun at exactly four, The Faker and Spyfall are built for that count and need less setup.

### Alibi

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Bluffing

**How to play:**

1. Two people are accused of the same crime and claim they were together elsewhere.
2. They get 60 seconds to quietly agree their story.
3. The other two are detectives and question each suspect separately (the other covers ears / hums), asking both the same questions.

**Win / lose:** any clear contradiction between the two suspects = guilty; matching answers all the way through = innocent.

**Bank (crimes):** ate the last snack, scratched the car, broke the aux cable, got us lost on purpose, set the alarm for the wrong time, left the fuel tank empty, ate someone else's reserved sandwich, hid the phone charger, booked the wrong hotel, lost the spare key, reversed into a bin, left the lights on and killed the battery, "borrowed" someone's hoodie and never gave it back, spilled coffee on the seat, deleted the road-trip playlist.

**Bank (detective questions, ask both and compare):**

- Whose idea was it to go there?
- Who drove / who paid?
- What was the weather doing?
- What were you wearing?
- What's the last thing you said to each other before you split up?
- Who else was there?
- What did you have to eat or drink?
- What song was playing?
- How long were you there, exactly?
- What did you touch / pick up?

### Secret Rule

**Players** 3-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Deduction

**How to play:** one person silently picks a secret rule. Going around, each other player says a word; the rule-keeper replies only "pass" or "fail." The rule must be checkable from the spelling of the word alone (never from what the word means), so there's never an argument — use the bank below.

**Win / lose:** first to correctly state the rule wins and becomes the next keeper.

**Bank (rules):**

- the word has a double letter (ba**ll**, co**ff**ee)
- the word starts and ends with the same letter (**l**eve**l**, **a**re**a**)
- the word starts with a vowel (apple, igloo)
- the word ends in a vowel (pizza, radio)
- the word contains the letter A
- the word contains the letter S
- the word has no letter from the word "ten" (no T, E or N anywhere)
- the word is exactly four letters long
- the word is longer than six letters
- the word ends in "-ing"
- the word contains a double vowel (b**oo**k, r**ai**n)
- your word starts with the same letter the previous player's word ended on
- the word starts with the same letter as the keeper's first name

### One Word Clue Chain

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Word

**How to play:**

1. One Guesser looks out the window and stays quiet.
2. The rest agree on a secret word (say it quietly or show it on a phone).
3. Going around, each clue-giver says one single word linked to it. No repeating a clue, no part of the target word, no rhyming, no gestures.
4. After each full lap the Guesser gets one guess.

**Win / lose:** Guesser names the word within 3 laps → the car wins; otherwise the word stumped them. (If two clue-givers give near-identical clues, the Guesser locks on too fast — come at it from different angles.)

**Example:** secret word "pirate" → "ship," "treasure," "eyepatch," "parrot," "ocean."

**Bank (words):** pirate, dentist, volcano, wedding, penguin, library, thunder, cowboy, vampire, airport, snowman, detective, pancake, robot, lighthouse, dragon, beekeeper, astronaut.

## Card Games

No table needed — hold cards in hand and use a lap or the console for a tiny draw/discard pile. The driver does not play card games.

### Rummy

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car if smooth, better at a stop · **Driver** Excluded · **Energy** Low · **Setup** Deck of cards · **Type** Card · **Time** 15-30 min

**How to play:** deal 7 cards each. Put the rest as a draw pile and turn one card face up as the discard pile. On your turn, draw the top of the draw pile or the top discard, then discard one card. Build melds in your hand: sets (three 8s) or runs (4-5-6 of hearts), kept hidden until going out. A player goes out when all cards are in valid melds except one final discard.

**Win / lose:** everyone else scores penalty points from cards left in hand; lowest total after a few rounds wins.

**Note:** calm and strategic; needs a small draw/discard pile on a lap or console. Skip if people want fast laughs.

### Crazy Eights

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Excluded · **Energy** Low · **Setup** Deck of cards · **Type** Card · **Time** 5-15 min

**How to play:** deal 5 cards each. Flip one card to start the discard pile. On your turn, play a card matching the top discard by suit or rank. Any 8 is wild — when you play it, name the next suit. If you can't play, draw one, then play it if possible or pass.

**Win / lose:** first player with no cards wins. Optional scoring: losers count card values left in hand (8s worth 50).

**Note:** the best moving-car card game — almost everything stays in hand and the shared piles are small.

### Hearts

**Players** 4 · **Where** Best at a stop · **Driver** Excluded · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** Deck of cards (people who know it) · **Type** Card · **Time** 25-45 min

**How to play:** deal all cards to 4 players. Each hand, pass 3 cards (rotate left, right, across, then no pass). The player with 2 of clubs leads. Follow the led suit if you can; otherwise play anything. Highest card of the led suit wins the trick and leads next. Hearts can't be led until "broken" by being discarded on another suit. Each heart = 1 penalty point, queen of spades = 13. Take all hearts and the queen ("shoot the moon") and everyone else gets 26.

**Win / lose:** lowest score wins.

**Note:** keep tricks as small face-down stacks. Only for players who already know trick-taking.

### Spades

**Players** 4 (2v2 teams) · **Where** Best at a stop · **Driver** Excluded · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** Deck of cards (people who know it) · **Type** Card · **Time** 30-45 min

**How to play:** two teams of 2, partners alternating. Deal all cards. Each player bids how many tricks they'll win; team bids combine. Follow suit if possible; otherwise play anything including a spade. Highest card of the led suit wins unless a spade is played, then highest spade wins. Spades can't be led until broken (unless you only have spades).

**Win / lose:** make your team bid → 10 points per bid trick + 1 per extra; miss → lose 10 per bid trick. First to an agreed score wins.

**Note:** rules-heavy — too much to teach casually in a moving car.

## Fast Competitive Games

Each has a hard way to lose every round, so nobody can coast. Each round crowns a winner; drop a point on the Trip Scoreboard if you want it to add up.

### Mind Meld

**Players** 2 at a time · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Word

**How to play:**

1. The pair counts "1, 2, 3" and each blurts one word — no planning.
2. They almost never match. Next round, both simultaneously say a word that bridges the two previous words (if it was "fire" and "ocean," both might aim for "steam"). You can't re-use any word already said this round.
3. Keep going, reading each other and converging.

**Win / lose:** the pair matches a word → win; no match within 6 rounds → fail, next pair up. Score = rounds taken (fewer is better).

### Contact

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car (best taught at a stop) · **Driver** Excluded (listens only); passengers run it · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None (timer) · **Type** Deduction

**How to play:**

1. The Keeper silently picks a secret word and says only its first letter (e.g. "B").
2. Guessers think of words starting with that letter. A guesser gives a clue to their candidate without naming it: "it's a yellow fruit."
3. If another guesser thinks they know it, they say "Contact!" Both count "3, 2, 1" and say their word at once.
4. If they match and it's not the Keeper's word, the Keeper must reveal the next letter ("BA"). The Keeper can defend: if the Keeper says the clued word before the count finishes, the clue is killed.

**Win / lose:** guessers win by saying the full word or forcing every letter out; the Keeper wins by running out a 4-minute timer.

**Example:** Keeper says "B." Guesser 1 clues "it's a yellow fruit" (thinking banana). Guesser 2 shouts "Contact!", they count 3-2-1 and both say "banana." It matches and isn't the Keeper's word, so the Keeper reveals "BL." (Had the Keeper said "banana?" before the count ended, the clue would've died.) Now everyone re-clues for a word starting "BL."

### Don't Say It

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** High · **Setup** None (timer) · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** one person sits in the hot seat. The group picks 2-3 forbidden words — always "yes" and "no," plus one wildcard ("I," a colour, "car"). For 60 seconds the group rapid-fires questions; the hot-seat player must answer every one, fast.

**Win / lose:** say a forbidden word, or dodge so hard you stop answering, and you're caught — note how long you lasted. Everyone takes a turn; longest survivor wins.

### Pass The Take

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** High · **Setup** None · **Type** Performance

**How to play:** someone names a topic and one person starts a strong opinion. Anyone can shout "switch!" and the next person must continue the exact same argument mid-sentence, same stance and momentum, as one continuous escalating rant.

**Win / lose:** stall, contradict the stance, repeat a point, or break the sentence → you're out. Last one ranting wins.

**Bank (topics):** why mornings are a scam, the correct way to load a dishwasher, why this is the best gas station snack, airports, why we should have left earlier, the problem with modern shoes, why this playlist is genius, the case against socks, why the back seat is the best seat, the truth about breakfast, why roundabouts are superior, the problem with other drivers, why we deserve a longer break, the dark side of group chats, why this is the last road trip we plan like this.

## Classic Word & Memory Games

Well-known games with no equipment and clear win/lose rules.

### Ghost

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Word

**How to play:**

1. Each player adds one letter to a growing fragment. Every letter must head toward a real word of 4+ letters.
2. You do not want to be the one who completes a real word — add letters that keep a word possible but push the completing letter onto the next person.
3. Instead of adding a letter you can challenge the previous player to name a real word starting with the fragment. If they can, you lose the round; if they can't, they do.

**Win / lose:** completing a word, or losing a challenge, earns you a letter — G, then H, O, S, T. Spell GHOST and you're out; last player standing wins.

**Example:** C → CL → CLO → CLOU (nearly forced to CLOUD/CLOUT) → next player must add D or T and completes the word.

**Harder version — Superghost:** add a letter to either end of the fragment (ERA → bERA or ERAd).

### Fizz Buzz

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Reflex

**How to play:** count up out loud around the car. Any number divisible by 3 → "Fizz." Divisible by 5 → "Buzz." Divisible by both (15, 30, 45…) → "Fizz Buzz." The count goes: 1, 2, Fizz, 4, Buzz, Fizz, 7, 8, Fizz, Buzz, 11, Fizz, 13, 14, Fizz Buzz, 16…

**Win / lose:** say the wrong thing or break the rhythm and you're out. Last player in wins; restart from 1.

**Bank (harder variants, pick one):**

- add a third rule: divisible by 7 → "Bang"
- any number that just contains a 3 (13, 23, 30-39) also becomes "Fizz," not only multiples
- reverse the direction of play on every "Buzz"
- put everyone on a half-second answer clock

### I'm Going on a Picnic

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Memory

**How to play:** first player: "I'm going on a picnic and I'm bringing apples." Each next player repeats the whole list in order and adds one new item.

**Win / lose:** miss an item, get the order wrong, or stall → you're out. Last player standing wins.

**Bank (frames & harder version):** swap the frame to keep it fresh — "In my suitcase I packed…", "At the festival I saw…", "On the road we passed…". Harder: items must be added in alphabetical order (apples, bread, cheese, dates…).

## Challenges & Punishments

Each challenge has one concrete way to fail (no judgment calls). Whoever fails rolls the Punishment Bank (a dice app set to 8 sides) or lets the catcher pick. Every punishment is demanding to keep up or something to do — never a one-off sentence. No punishment costs money, and none is about ranking or roasting each other.

Driver only ever draws 1-5 (talk-only). 6-7 are passenger-only. 8 is stop-only. Keep a failure tally on the Trip Scoreboard; **most failures by arrival does the Finale** — the group picks one Walk of Shame look (#8) to wear for the first hour at the destination, or the loser does the Narrator (#5) on the group for the first 15 minutes after parking.

1. **Voice for a leg** *(driver-safe)* — until the next stop you may only talk in an assigned voice; slipping into your normal voice = re-draw. Pick one: movie-trailer narrator, GPS sat-nav lady, football commentator, toddler, Bond villain, nature-documentary narrator, 1920s radio announcer, Shakespearean actor, ASMR whisper, robot/text-to-speech, pirate, news anchor, gym motivational coach, gravelly action hero.
2. **The Catchphrase** *(driver-safe)* — until the next stop, end every sentence with an assigned tag. Pick one: "…and I stand by that," "…allegedly," "…as a man of culture," "…sadly," "…for legal reasons," "…probably," "…like my father before me," "…and that's the tea," "…no further questions," "…respectfully," "…in this economy?", "…God willing," "…as the prophecy foretold," "…but that's none of my business," "…trust the process," "…and I won't apologise."
3. **The Trigger** *(driver-safe)* — do an assigned reaction every time you pass a roadside trigger, until the next stop; miss one and the clock resets. Trigger: every truck / bridge or tunnel / fuel or fast-food sign / roundabout / sign for another city / police car. Reaction (driver = voice only): cheer "LET'S GO," say "thank you for your service," gasp, mutter "…and there it is," or boo. Passengers may add hands up, a salute, or a knee drumroll.
4. **Sound-Effects Guy** *(driver-safe)* — for 10 minutes, add a mouth sound effect to every action in the car (door creak, a glug when someone drinks, tick-tock for the blinker, a whoosh for a phone grab). Miss an obvious one and the 10 minutes restart.
5. **The Narrator** *(driver-safe)* — for 10 minutes narrate the car like a hushed nature documentary. Go quiet more than 30 seconds or break = restart.
6. **One-Handed** *(passenger-only)* — tuck your dominant hand under your leg; everything one-handed until the next stop. Caught using both = re-draw.
7. **Frozen** *(passenger-only)* — sit on both hands for 5 minutes while the others bait you (offer snacks, fake-toss things). Move your arms = fail.
8. **Walk of Shame** *(stop-only)* — walk into the next stop wearing an assigned look (using only what's in the car) and keep it until you're back inside. Pick one: sunglasses indoors, hood up with the drawstring fully shut, jacket backwards, collar pulled over your head, trousers tucked into socks, or an exaggerated catwalk strut.

### The Interrogation

**Players** 4 (one in the chair) · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** one person is in the chair; the others read questions off the list, fast. You must answer out loud within 3 seconds, and may not say "I don't know," "pass," "maybe," or change the subject. The answer needn't be good — just instant.

**Win / lose:** stall past a 3-count, dodge, or non-answer → punishment, next person takes the chair.

**Bank (questions):**

- What's a food you pretend to like?
- If you had to delete one app forever, which?
- What's the worst movie you secretly enjoy?
- Weirdest thing you've ever eaten?
- What's the last thing you searched on your phone?
- What animal would you fight for 1000 euros?
- Worst haircut you've ever had?
- What's your most useless talent?
- What's a word you can never spell right?
- Worst gift you've ever received?
- What's massively overrated?
- What's your comfort meal at 2am?
- What chore do you hate the most?
- What trend do you completely not get?
- Pineapple on pizza — defend your answer?
- Cats or dogs, and why is the other one worse?
- Dumbest way you've ever injured yourself?
- What skill do you wish you had?
- Window or aisle, no hesitating?
- What's the worst advice you've ever followed?
- A famous person you find weirdly annoying?
- What would you do with a completely free Saturday?
- What's something you're irrationally scared of?
- Best thing you've ever eaten at a gas station?
- What's a hill you'd genuinely die on?

### Questions Only

**Players** 2 at a time · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** two players act out a quick scene, speaking only in questions, back and forth.

**Win / lose:** say a statement, hesitate over 3 seconds, repeat a question, or throw a non-question ("uh, what?") → out. Loser draws a punishment; winner faces a new challenger.

**Bank (opening lines):** "Do you come here often?" / "Is that really what you're wearing?" / "Have you seen my keys?" / "Why are you in my house?" / "Are you going to finish that?" / "Did you just take my parking spot?"

**Bank (scenarios):** two strangers at a bus stop, a terrible job interview, a couple lost and arguing about directions, two spies who half-recognise each other, a customer and the world's worst waiter, two people who both think the other called the meeting.

### The Forbidden Letter

**Players** 1-2 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None (timer) · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** you're assigned a common letter. For 5 minutes (set a timer) you may not say any word containing it, while everyone else asks you questions to trip you up. Best as a duel — two people under the same letter, last to slip wins.

**Win / lose:** say a word containing the letter and get caught → punishment.

**Bank (letters by difficulty):** Warm-up: B, F, K, P, G, V. Real challenge: D, H, M, W, L, C. Nightmare: S, T, R, N. Don't ban a vowel — that makes speech impossible. Hardest fair version: ban S for a 90-second burst.

### The Rhyme Chain

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** someone calls a starting word; going around, each person says a new word that rhymes, within a 3-second count.

**Win / lose:** can't produce one in time, repeat a word, or try a fake rhyme (group vetoes) → out for the round. Last standing wins; whoever went out first draws a punishment.

**Bank (starter words):** cat, light, day, four, blue, sound, name, cold, train, line, top, bear, cake, hill, ring, sing, gold, mad, school, tall.

### Keep a Straight Face

**Players** 2+ (one target) · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None (timer) · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** one person holds a straight face for 60 seconds while a challenger reads the lines below in the most deadpan voice possible.

**Win / lose:** the target laughs, smiles, or cracks a grin (car judges) → punishment.

**Bank (deadpan lines):**

- "I have never once trusted a man who folds his pizza."
- "My greatest enemy in this world is the second sock."
- "I would like to formally apologise to every escalator I have ever stood still on."
- "Sometimes I clap when the plane lands. Alone. In my apartment."
- "I believe geese know exactly what they did."
- "My five-year plan begins with buying a slightly bigger spoon."
- "Every cloud is just a sky burrito waiting to happen."
- "I once lost a staring contest to a fish and I think about it daily."
- "I don't believe in Tuesdays and I never have."
- "A pigeon looked me in the eye last week and I haven't felt safe since."
- "I keep my cereal in the fridge and my milk in my heart."
- "I have a complicated relationship with revolving doors."
- "I would trust a horse to do my taxes before I'd trust most men."
- "Sometimes I whisper 'good luck' to my food before I eat it."
- "I have never finished a tube of lip balm and I never will."
- "My greatest fear is being asked to guess someone's age."

### Order Roulette

**Players** 1 (rest witness) · **Where** At a stop · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** at a food or coffee stop, draw an order style and keep it up for the whole order, with the group within earshot.

**Win / lose:** drop the style, laugh out of it, or order normally → punishment.

**Bank (order styles):**

- Order entirely in a whisper.
- Call the cashier "captain" at least twice.
- Order your drink by describing it, never its name — "the dark hot bean water," "the cold yellow fizzy one."
- Add "…if you'd be so kind" to the end of every request.
- Order like it's the most important decision of your life, then change your mind twice.
- Order for an imaginary table of ten, then finish with "actually, just the one."
- Refer to every item as "the usual."
- Speak only in a hushed, dramatic movie-trailer voice.
- Be aggressively, suspiciously over-polite about everything.
- Pretend you're a regular and act mildly hurt they don't recognise you.
- Ask one genuine but very specific question about every item before ordering.
- Treat the cashier like a long-lost friend you're thrilled to see.

### No-Complaint Stretch

**Players** All · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** pick one leg between stops; nobody may complain. A complaint = any negative remark about the trip, comfort, music, food, timing, traffic, other drivers, weather, or saying you're tired/hungry/bored/need the toilet. Neutral questions are fine; whining is not.

**Win / lose:** first person to complain (group judges) → punishment.

### The Banned Word

**Players** All · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** the group picks one extremely common word that's hard to avoid for the whole leg; anyone who says it gets caught.

**Win / lose:** say the banned word and get caught → punishment, or run it as a tally where the most slips by the next stop draws.

**Bank (banned-word options):** "like," "literally," "actually," "honestly," "basically," "bro," "dude," "just," "so," "I think," "kind of," "you know," "no cap," or everyone's first names.

### Tongue Twister Gauntlet

**Players** 1 at a time · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** you're assigned a tongue twister and must say it three times fast in a row.

**Win / lose:** stumble, mangle a word, or grind to a crawl (car judges) → punishment.

**Bank (twisters):**

- unique New York

### The Minister's Cat

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** going around the car, describe "the minister's cat" with an adjective, all on the same letter, starting at A ("an angry cat," "an awesome cat"). Once everyone's gone, move to B, then C, through the alphabet.

**Win / lose:** can't produce an adjective within a 3-count, or repeat one already used → out (or draw a punishment). Last player standing wins.

**Harder version:** cumulative — repeat every adjective said so far, in order, then add the next letter's.

### The Counting Game (21)

**Players** 2-4 · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Medium · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**How to play:** count upward around the car. On your turn say one, two, or three numbers in a row.

**Win / lose:** whoever is forced to say 21 is out and draws a punishment; restart from 1.

**Harder version:** each round's winner adds a rule replacing a number with an action (7 = a clap, 14 = "moo", any multiple of 5 = reverse direction). Break a rule = out. Rules stack into chaos.

## Long-Running Challenges (whole-trip)

These simmer in the background for hours or the whole drive rather than finishing in one round. Track them on the Trip Scoreboard; the loser draws a Punishment Bank punishment (or does the Finale) at the destination.

### Don't Get Got (Secret Missions)

**Players** 3-4 · **Where** In car & at stops · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** Phone or paper (deal missions) · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** the whole trip — it plays in the background of everything else.

**How to play:** each player secretly gets 2-3 missions, each one a small trick to make another person do something without realising it's a setup. Pull it off, then reveal it to score. If someone suspects you mid-attempt and accuses you ("are you trying to get me?"), you must admit it and that mission is dead.

**Win / lose:** first to complete 3 missions wins; a caught mission is burned.

**Bank (car-friendly missions):**

- get someone to smell something ("does this smell weird to you?")
- get someone to repeat a weird word back to you
- get a player to count something out loud for you
- get someone to hold your phone or drink for 10 seconds
- get someone to say a specific everyday word (e.g. "moist," "Belgium")
- get someone to high-five you for no real reason
- get someone to look behind them ("did you see that?")
- get a player to spell a word out loud
- get someone to swap seats with you at a stop
- get someone to read a road sign out loud
- get a player to agree to a fake plan ("we should totally do that")
- get someone to do an impression
- get someone to guess a number you're thinking of
- get a player to "teach" you a fact you already know
- get someone to check whether your seatbelt is clicked

### The Peg Game

**Players** All · **Where** In car & at stops · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None (tally on the Scoreboard) · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** the whole trip — a steal-points war in the background of everything.

**How to play:** before you set off, pick 1-3 forbidden words bound to come up (see bank). For the rest of the trip, if you catch someone saying a forbidden word, call it and take a point off them onto your own tally. (The party version uses clothespins you physically steal; in a car a tally on the Trip Scoreboard works fine.) This is the all-trip cousin of The Banned Word, which runs for a single leg and ends in a punishment.

**Win / lose:** most points at the destination wins.

**Bank (forbidden words):** the destination's name, "are we there yet," "tired," "hungry," "phone," "music," "stop," "actually," "bro," "literally," "I'm just saying," "to be fair," "no offence," each other's first names, a chosen filler word.

### Assassin

**Players** 4 · **Where** In car & at stops · **Driver** Plays · **Energy** Low · **Setup** Paper or phone (assign targets) · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** the whole trip — a quiet elimination chain.

**How to play:** secretly assign each player one other player as their target, forming a loop (one person deals it on paper or by message). The whole group plays with the **same single "kill" trigger** for the game (pick one from the bank). You assassinate your target by getting them to do that trigger without realising you engineered it; pull it off, quietly tell them "you're out," and you inherit their target. If a target catches you in the act ("you're trying to get me"), the attempt fails and you must wait a few minutes before trying again. (Unlike Don't Get Got — many varied missions, first to three — Assassin is one target and one method at a time, last-man-standing.)

**Win / lose:** last assassin standing wins.

**Bank (pick one trigger for the whole game):**

- get your target to accept food or gum from your hand
- get your target to say "yes" three times within a minute
- get your target to repeat a specific agreed word back to you
- get your target to give you a high-five
- get your target to read something out loud for you
- get your target to touch their own nose or ear

### Road Trip Bingo

**Players** All · **Where** In car · **Driver** Plays (calls a spot, a passenger marks it) · **Energy** Low · **Setup** Phone or paper (a grid each) · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** a long leg or the whole trip.

**How to play:** each player makes a grid (4x4 for a leg, 5x5 for the whole trip) of roadside things from the bank, shuffled so the cards differ. Mark a square when you genuinely spot that thing out the window.

**Win / lose:** first to a full line (or a full card, for the long game) shouts "Bingo!" and wins.

**Bank (squares):** cow, horse, tractor, police car, broken-down car, caravan or camper, hitchhiker, roadworks, tunnel, big bridge, water tower, wind turbine, church, McDonald's sign, petrol station, hay bales, a dog in another car, a foreign number plate, a "for sale" sign, a speed camera, a rest area, a lorry with a funny logo, someone asleep in another car, a flag, a castle or ruin, a lake or river, a person on a bike, a caravan towing trouble.

### Cows on My Side

**Players** Passengers · **Where** In car · **Driver** Excluded (eyes on the road) · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** the whole trip — a slow-burn point war.

**How to play:** when you spot livestock on your side of the car, shout "Cows on my side!" before anyone else and score a point per group of them. Spot them on someone else's side and call "Cows on your side!" first to deny them the point. Two running twists: pass a cemetery → shout "Ghost cows!" to wipe out the current leader's points; pass a church → "Church bells!" doubles your own.

**Win / lose:** most points by the destination wins.

### Padiddle

**Players** Passengers · **Where** In car (after dark) · **Driver** Excluded · **Energy** Low · **Setup** None · **Type** Challenge

**Runs:** all evening.

**How to play:** be the first to shout "Padiddle!" when you spot a car with only one working headlight.

**Win / lose:** a correct call scores a point; call it on a motorbike (which only has one light anyway) and you lose one. Most points by the end of the night wins.
