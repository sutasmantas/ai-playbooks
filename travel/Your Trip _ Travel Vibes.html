Day 2 Food Tips:

  • Pack a small bag: sandwiches, fruit, energy bars — saves ~€10–15 vs park food

  • Park has meal deals inside (look for combo offers at main restaurants)

  • After park: Oberża Zatorska in Zator for a solid Polish dinner before the drive

Energylandia Insider Tips:

  • Energy Pass (~219–229 PLN) gives fast-lane access to all top coasters — worth it on busy days

  • Avoid Sundays and Polish public holidays (busiest days)

  • Bring a small locker-friendly bag — you'll need to stow items on major coasters

  • Height restrictions: Hyperion 140cm+ | Zadra 140cm+

BUDGET BREAKDOWN

Estimated Total: ~€120–180 per person (3 days)

Fixed / Booked

  • Energylandia ticket: ~€50 (229 PLN)

  • Energylandia parking: ~€7 (30 PLN)

Variable Estimates

  • Fuel: ~€60–80 (full loop Kaunas–Kraków–Mountains–Kaunas, petrol car)

  • Accommodation: ~€40–90 (2 nights, budget options)

  • Food: ~€20–35 (mix of supermarket + cheap local spots)

  • Kraków activities: ~€0–15 (market square & castle hill = free!)

  • Mountain entry/hiking: ~€0–5

Budget Hacks

  • Biedronka / Lidl for road trip snacks — huge saving over motorway stops

  • Kazimierz milk bars (bar mleczny) for ~€3–5 full meals

  • Wawel hill grounds = free in evenings

  • Book Energylandia online (sometimes cheaper than gate price)

PACKING LIST

Essentials

  • Energylandia e-tickets (downloaded to phone)

  • Car documents + driving licence

  • Vignette/toll pass if needed (Poland = free motorways for cars! )

  • Phone mount + charger for navigation

  • Portable water bottle + snack bag

Clothing

  • Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones in Kraków are brutal)

  • Change of clothes after water park (Day 2)

  • Light jacket — evenings in Kraków can be cool

  • Rain layer (especially for mountains, Day 3)

  • Hiking shoes if doing Morskie Oko (option A)

Nice to Have

  • Small dry bag / locker bag for coaster rides

  • Sunscreen (long park day!)

  • Cash (PLN) — some Zakopane places are cash-only
